Why teams switch to Metric

Most analytics tools tell you what already happened. Metric tells you what's happening now — and what's about to.

01
Streaming

Every event, the moment it fires

Metric ingests events with sub-second latency and never samples your data. Whether you send ten events a second or ten million, every click, view, and conversion lands in your dashboards before the user's next tap.

No nightly batch jobs, no "your data will be ready in 24 hours." Query live traffic the same way you query last quarter — one engine, one schema, zero waiting.

02
Behavior

See the path, not just the pageview

Pageviews tell you what happened. Metric shows you the sequence that led there — the funnels users complete, the steps where they stall, and the exact screen where a session goes quiet.

Build a funnel in three clicks, break it down by plan, device, or cohort, and watch conversion move as you ship. Every drop-off is a link straight to the sessions behind it.

03
Signals

Catch churn while you can still stop it

Retention is decided long before a customer cancels. Metric watches for the early signals — declining sessions, ignored features, rising support friction — and scores every account for risk in real time.

Route at-risk users into a lifecycle campaign, a CSM's queue, or a Slack alert automatically. By the time the renewal date arrives, the intervention has already happened.

04
Infrastructure

Runs on your warehouse, not a black box

Metric queries the data you already own — BigQuery, Snowflake, Redshift, or Postgres — so your events never leave your infrastructure and your bill never scales with how many people log in.

Model events in plain SQL, version the definitions in git, and expose them to the whole company through dashboards non-engineers can actually read. Your definitions, your governance, your data.
